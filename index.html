What is POSP in Insurance (Point Of Sales Person)?

The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) oversees and regulates the insurance sector in India. To make insurance more accessible, IRDAI introduced the POSP (Point of Sales Person) initiative, allowing individuals to sell insurance after obtaining certification.
The full form of POSP is “Point Of Sales Person”.  A POSP insurance agent is a certified professional, licensed by IRDAI who helps people buy insurance from companies. They act as a bridge between customers and insurance companies who explain different policies and help people choose the right insurance plan for their needs. They also answer customer questions and provide guidance on policy benefits.

Don’t find the answer? We can help

POSP stands for Point of Sales Person. Introduced by IRDAI, it is a certification pathway that allows individuals to sell insurance products after completing the required training and examination.

A POSP is an IRDAI-certified insurance agent who helps customers understand, compare, and purchase insurance policies. They act as a trusted bridge between insurers and buyers by explaining coverage, answering questions, and guiding people to the right plan.

To become an insurance agent, you need to complete training and pass an exam. After that, you can start selling policies to customers.

You should have completed at least Class 10 (or the equivalent qualification prescribed by IRDAI), along with good communication skills and a willingness to help customers choose suitable insurance products.

The minimum age to become a POSP insurance agent in India is 18 years. Candidates must also complete mandatory training and clear the IRDAI certification exam before they can start selling policies.
